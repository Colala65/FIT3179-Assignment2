Meat Exports by Country (1990–2020)

Although China produces a lot of meat, they do not export as much as other countries.

Both Brazil and United States are the largest overall exporters of meat.

In 2020, Brazil has the largest exports of beef and poultry, while United States also has large exports on pig meat and poultry.

New Zealand and Australia have consistently been the top exporters of sheep.

Consumption per person and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita

GDP per capita can be used as a way to evaluate the standard of living.

While having a higher GDP per capita does not directly correspond to higher consumptions of meat per person, a general upwards trend in consumption can be seen with an increasing GDP per capita.

In 2017, Australia and the United States have the two highest consumptions of meat per person.

Years starting from 2021 and onwards are projections.

Beginning from 1990, Pig meat is the most consumed type of meat globally. Going into 2017, Poultry surpasses Pig meat as the most consumed, indicating an increase in the popularity of chicken.

After 2018, there is a quite a bit of a drop in the total consumption of pig.
